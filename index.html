How we build

A dark store, not a retail store.

A dark store is a fulfilment-only warehouse. No customers walk in. It is designed for one job. Packing orders and getting them out the door, fast.

A real-world warning

Why we build the tech ourselves,
not hire it out.

Every grocery delivery business that has won at scale owns its own software. Sixty60, Ocado in the UK, Picnic in the Netherlands, Tesco. There is a reason.

Reason 1
When something breaks, one team owns it.
Cold chain fails. Payment doesn't go through. The order is wrong. With a third-party developer you spend more time arguing about whose fault it is than fixing the problem. With owned software, one team is responsible. End of conversation.
Reason 2
Speed of change matters every week.
A grocery operation needs software changes based on what the warehouse floor sees today. A third party ships changes in months. We ship them in days. That gap is the whole game.
Reason 3
Customer data belongs inside the business.
A third-party platform holds your customer data, not you. You can't build a real loyalty programme, real personalisation, or your own marketing on someone else's database. This is the long-term asset.
The grocery winners globally are all owners: Sixty60 (Shoprite-owned), Ocado, Picnic, Tesco. Aggregator and third-party apps have not won this category in any major market.
